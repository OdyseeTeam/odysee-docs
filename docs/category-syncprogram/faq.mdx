# Frequently Asked Questions

Here, we give you answers to the most frequently asked questions about the sync program.

<FAQ>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>What Does Odysee do With My Channel?</strong>
    </summary>
    The only thing syncing to Odysee authorizes us to do, and all that we're
    interested in doing, is collect the metadata from your channel and its
    content to sync it to Odysee.
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>Will Syncing Deboost My Channel?</strong>
    </summary>
    No. Odysee has tens of thousands of synced YouTube channels, and we have
    never heard of anyone being banned, shadowbanned, or otherwise punished for
    syncing. In fact, we've seen several cases of channels skyrocketing in
    popularity after syncing to Odysee, which wouldn't be possible had syncing
    deboosted them.
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>Why Are There Sync Limits and Requirements?</strong>
    </summary>
    If it was up to us, we would allow everyone to sync with no restrictions. As
    it stands now, we have limited resources for channel syncs and have to
    allocate them as best we can.
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>
        Can I use the Odysee Sync Tool (OST) even if I do not meet the channel
        sync requirements?
      </strong>
    </summary>
    Yes. The Odysee Sync Tool (OST) can be used by any creator, regardless of
    subscriber count. If you do not qualify for the auto sync program, you can
    still use OST to bring your content over to Odysee yourself.
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>
        Is the Odysee Sync Tool (OST) available on Android or iOS?
      </strong>
    </summary>
    No. The Odysee Sync Tool (OST) is currently only available for desktop
    operating systems. It can be used on Windows, Linux, and macOS, but it is
    not available for Android or iOS devices.
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>Can I Undo My Sync?</strong>
    </summary>
    Yes. There's no contract, hidden obligations, or anything else nefarious
    involved in the YouTube sync program. Your content remains 100% yours, and
    you can opt-out at anytime. You can do this by deleting the channel
    entirely, or by requesting an end to your sync. This would keep your channel
    and all its current content, but prevent new content from syncing. You can
    request a sync cancellation by emailing{" "}
    <a href="mailto:hello@odysee.com.">hello@odysee.com</a>.
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>Can I sync a channel again after deleting it?</strong>
    </summary>
    It is not possible to sync a channel to Odysee once the channel owner has
    deleted it or created a new account. You can request more information about
    this by sending an email to hello@odysee.com.
  </details>
  <details>
    <summary>
      <strong>
        When should I use the auto sync program instead of the Odysee Sync Tool
        (OST)?
      </strong>
    </summary>
    The auto sync program is intended for channels that qualify under the
    current sync requirements and want Odysee to handle the sync process. OST is
    a self-service option for creators who want to migrate content themselves,
    including creators who do not meet the auto sync thresholds.
  </details>
</FAQ>
